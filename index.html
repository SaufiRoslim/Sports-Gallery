sports

A competitive physical activity is referred to as sport. Sport is often defined as physical activities requiring athleticism or dexterity. Sports are frequently governed by rules to ensure that the competition is fair and that the victor is determined consistently. Performance records are frequently kept and publicised in sports news. Sport is a key source of entertainment, with spectator sports attracting enormous crowds and sports broadcasting reaching a wider audience.

Football

Few sports can match football as a spectator sport, and the number of people who participate in the sport, whether at the grassroots level, in 5-a-side leagues, or simply for fun with friends, is unrivalled. According to the most recent global census conducted by FIFA, the sport has 265 million participants and more than 5 million referees, accounting for 4% of the world's population.

Badminton

Some people may be surprised to learn that badminton is one of the top ten most popular sports in the world. However, badminton is a very popular indoor sport, with an estimated 220 million people playing it on a regular basis. It is especially popular in Asia, where many of the best players in the game's history have come from.

Basketball

The Federation of International Basketball Associations (FIBA) estimates that 450 million people worldwide participate in basketball, whether recreationally or in organised competitions. Basketball legends such as Michael Jordan and Kobe Bryant have aided the sport's global popularity as it has grown from its American roots.

Volley Ball

Volleyball, which originated in America, has a staggering global participation figure of 998 million people. Volleyball is played indoors and outdoors all over the world, with over 220 affiliated national federations registered with the sport's global governing body, the 'FIVB.'
